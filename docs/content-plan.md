# StickerFit Studio Content Plan

## AdSense Readiness Checklist

Use this checklist before applying or reapplying for Google AdSense.

- The site is reachable on a production domain with HTTPS.
- The homepage provides a useful tool, not only thin informational content.
- Public pages include About, Contact, Privacy, and Terms.
- The public contact email is domain-based and forwards to a verified mailbox.
- `robots.txt` allows crawling and points to the sitemap.
- `sitemap.xml` lists the homepage, guide pages, and trust pages.
- Content is original, helpful, and written for sticker sellers.
- Navigation lets users reach the calculator, guides, and trust pages.
- Pages do not contain demo-only notes, placeholder language, personal emails, or local machine paths.
- AdSense code and `ads.txt` should be added only after a publisher ID is available.

## Content Strategy

The site should not publish generic craft articles. Every article should support one of three business goals:

1. Attract search visitors with high-intent sticker production questions.
2. Move visitors into the calculator workflow.
3. Build trust for AdSense review, future affiliate links, and future paid features.

## Publishing Cadence

- First review batch: 8 guide pages plus core calculator and trust pages.
- Ongoing cadence: 2-4 high-quality guides per month.
- Refresh cadence: update size and pricing guides whenever tool presets, platform fees, or production recommendations change.

## Affiliate Preparation

Affiliate content should only be added after the site has useful non-commercial content. Recommended categories:

- Sticker paper and vinyl sheets.
- Laminate and overlay sheets.
- Cutting mats, blades, and weeding tools.
- Printer supplies and ink management.
- Packaging materials for sticker shops.

Each affiliate page must include a clear disclosure near the first affiliate link and avoid claiming a product is best unless the claim is supported by direct testing or transparent criteria.

## Subscription Preparation

Do not build subscriptions before traffic and user intent are clear. Candidate paid features:

- Saved sticker mixes and cost presets.
- Multi-layout comparison.
- Batch production records.
- PDF production sheets and quoting exports.
- Shop-level margin history.
- Expanded cutter preset library.

The simplest paid experiment should come before a full account system, for example a downloadable pricing workbook, printable production checklist, or lightweight Pro feature waitlist.
